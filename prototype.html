Design Alternatives

Comparison of different design approaches and rationale behind the chosen solution.

Alternative A: Traditional Menu-Based Interface

Linear progression with standard menu navigation

Key Features:
  • Sequential level-based progression
  • Standard dropdown menus for navigation
  • Text-heavy information display
  • Minimal visual feedback
Not Selected Too rigid and lacks engagement for modern students

Alternative B: Gamified Interactive Map

Visual exploration with game-like mechanics

Key Features:
  • Interactive campus map with hotspots
  • Real-time stat tracking and feedback
  • Visual character progression
  • Choice-based narrative system
Selected Best balance of engagement and educational value

Alternative C: Social Network Style Interface

Connection-focused with peer interaction emphasis

Key Features:
  • Profile-based navigation
  • Social networking elements
  • Peer comparison and ranking
  • Community-driven content
Not Selected May create unnecessary competition and stress

Why Alternative B Was Chosen

Alternative B (Gamified Interactive Map) was selected because it best addresses the core requirements of creating a playful and engaging learning experience while maintaining educational value. This approach:

  • Enhances Engagement: Interactive elements and visual feedback keep students motivated throughout their journey
  • Supports Learning: Clear cause-and-effect relationships help students understand the impact of their decisions
  • Reduces Stress: Game-like mechanics make the complex postgraduate preparation process feel manageable
  • Provides Personalization: Students can explore different paths based on their individual goals and interests
  • Offers Meaningful Feedback: Real-time stat tracking helps students monitor their progress and adjust their strategies
