Motivation & Research

The Why: We Chose the Active Track

The XJTLU Journey Simulator was developed under the Active Track because we identified a critical gap in how undergraduate students at XJTLU prepare for their postgraduate applications. Through our research, we discovered that many students feel overwhelmed by the complexity of the postgraduate application process, uncertain about which universities and programs to target, and anxious about managing their time effectively between academic preparation and personal well-being.

We chose the Active Track because it allows us to create an engaging, game-based learning experience that transforms the stressful application process into an interactive journey. Unlike passive information resources, our simulator enables students to experience different scenarios, make decisions, and witness the consequences in a safe environment.

Furthermore, the Active Track enables us to incorporate elements of gamification—such as progress tracking, achievement systems, and multiple endings—which naturally motivate students to engage deeply with the material.

The Gap: Literature & Product Review

Academic Papers Reviewed

Landers & Callan (2011) - Gamification in Education
What They Did Well
  • Established theoretical framework for gamification effects
  • Measured both intrinsic and extrinsic motivation
  • Provided empirical evidence for engagement increase
What They Missed
  • Lack of longitudinal studies on retention
  • Limited focus on emotional aspects
  • No specific implementation guidelines
Wang & Liu (2012) - Game-Based Learning
What They Did Well
  • Compared GBL vs traditional instruction
  • Measured learning outcomes quantitatively
  • Identified optimal game design principles
What They Missed
  • Ignored individual learning differences
  • Limited sample diversity
  • No long-term impact assessment
Reys et al. (2018) - Decision Making in Games
What They Did Well
  • Studied cognitive load in decision-making
  • Provided framework for choice architecture
  • Identified emotional factors in decisions
What They Missed
  • Lack of real-world application context
  • No consideration of time pressure
  • Missing stakeholder perspectives
Wouters et al. (2013) - UX in Educational Games
What They Did Well
  • Comprehensive UX evaluation methodology
  • Balanced fun and learning aspects
  • Considered player motivation types
What They Missed
  • Did not address anxiety reduction
  • Limited cultural context consideration
  • Lacked mobile/online implementation

Commercial Products Reviewed

Unigo - College Recruitment Game
What They Did Well
  • Engaging narrative-driven experience
  • Real university partnership for authenticity
  • Social media integration features
What They Missed
  • Focuses only on US universities
  • Lacks time management simulation
  • No mental health/well-being tracking
Scholly - Scholarship App
What They Did Well
  • Personalized scholarship matching
  • Clean, intuitive interface
  • Progress tracking system
What They Missed
  • No simulation of application process
  • Lacks decision consequence modeling
  • No gamification elements
ApplyKit - Application Tracker
What They Did Well
  • Comprehensive deadline management
  • Document organization features
  • Multi-university comparison tools
What They Missed
  • No interactive learning elements
  • Lacks simulation of outcomes
  • Doesn't address stress/anxiety
Edsby - Learning Platform
What They Did Well
  • Strong analytics and reporting
  • Real-time communication features
  • Integration with existing systems
What They Missed
  • Not specifically for postgraduate prep
  • Lacks game-based elements
  • Overly complex for students

The Stakeholders: Personas

We identified primary and secondary users with distinct personas to guide our design process:

PrimaryXJTLU Students

L
Ziheng Li

21 years old · Junior · Information and Computing Science

Primary UserTech-savvy

Background: Ziheng Li is a third-year undergraduate majoring in Information and Computing Science at XJTLU. He is actively preparing for postgraduate studies but feels overwhelmed by the choices and requirements.

Goals: Get into a top UK CS program, build strong technical portfolio, balance academic performance with extracurricular activities

Pain Points: Confused by multiple requirements, anxious about time management, feels overwhelmed by competition for top universities

Needs: Clear roadmap, realistic simulation, feedback on decisions

How the Simulator Helps: The XJTLU Journey Simulator helps Ziheng by allowing him to test different approaches to building his profile, providing feedback on the impact of his decisions, and helping him develop a more strategic approach to his postgraduate preparation.

C
Zhenyu Chen

20 years old · Sophomore · Electrical Engineering

Primary UserNovice

Background: Zhenyu Chen is a second-year undergraduate majoring in Electrical Engineering at XJTLU. He is starting to think about postgraduate studies but has limited knowledge about the application process.

Goals: Explore options, find right postgraduate direction, learn about the postgraduate application process, improve academic performance

Pain Points: No clear direction, overwhelmed by choices, uncertain about how to prepare for postgraduate applications

Needs: Simplified guidance, low-pressure exploration, understand requirements

How the Simulator Helps: The XJTLU Journey Simulator helps Zhenyu by providing a realistic simulation of the postgraduate application process, allowing him to experiment with different strategies and learn from the outcomes without real-world consequences.
